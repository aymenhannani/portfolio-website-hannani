The problem I solve

The numbers already exist. They're just scattered, rebuilt by hand, and quietly disagreeing with each other.

In most organisations, reporting lives across systems, spreadsheets and teams that were never designed to agree. Figures get rebuilt manually every month, two departments answer the same question differently, and nobody is quite sure which version to act on.

The real cost isn't the hours spent producing reports — it's the decisions made on numbers people don't fully trust, and the tools that quietly go unused after launch. I build the layer that fixes that: the pipelines, models and dashboards that agree with each other, plus the governance and training that keep them that way.

How I work

From scattered sources to something teams rely on

The same four steps, whether it's a group-wide platform or a single reporting stream.

  1. Discovery

    Understand the current state, the data sources, and what isn't visible today.

  2. Design

    Define the architecture, the data model, and the governance approach.

  3. Build

    Pipelines, dashboards or the platform itself, built and validated against the business.

  4. Adoption

    Training and documentation, so the solution is actually used and not just shipped.
